OpenGL projects

These two projects were my introduction to openGL and computer graphics in general. There are endless possibilities when it comes to draw things on the screen from games and simulations to drawing/modeling softwares and applications.

Space Racing Game

This game was completely developed in Legacy, the 20 year old api that doesn’t let me worry about code and maths behind. My only job was to call specific functions and it made me wonder how lighting works, what is culling or modelView matrix.

Of course, all these stuff are computed on the cpu side, so it doesn’t look as great as a AAA game. The protagonist is a TIE Fighter (Because I like Star Wars) that needs to finish the race under the strict watch of the Emperor. Here is a video that shows some graphics techniques used in the game:

Lawn Bowl Game

Modern openGL open my eyes to what computer graphics actually means and it looks much prettier than the project above. This introduction to shaders revealed the whole rasterization pipeline from vertex to fragment shader.

It was much more difficult as I had to handle this process of sending data to the gpu and manage to draw on the screen. . New things I came across like: matrices and how important they are, lighting models, mapping/texturing and more.

My aim was to get closer to the creation of a Game Engine. The code was structured in the way I imagined a Game Engine.
